About Us

Who we are and what we do

Overview

The Asian Music Psychology and Neuroscience Network (AMPNN) brings together scientists, clinicians, educators, industry professionals, and students from diverse fields including neuroscience, psychology, music, cognitive science, computer science, artificial intelligence, and medicine. Our goal is to advance the understanding of music’s profound impacts on the human brain, well-being, and cognitive development, with practical extensions into clinical and technological applications. We achieve this by facilitating research collaborations, providing educational resources, and organizing events such as workshops, seminars, and conferences across Asia. By promoting public awareness and encouraging knowledge exchange, AMPNN fosters a vibrant interdisciplinary community committed to uncovering the therapeutic, educational, and cognitive benefits of music.

Our Mission

To enhance scientific knowledge of music's role in cognitive function, emotional well-being, and neurological health, and to effectively communicate this knowledge to the public through interdisciplinary research, education, collaboration, and outreach across Asia.

What We Do

  • Conduct and support high-quality research in music psychology and neuroscience.
  • Facilitate interdisciplinary collaborations across diverse fields.
  • Organize conferences, workshops, and seminars to share knowledge and promote collaboration.
  • Provide educational resources and professional development opportunities.
  • Raise public awareness of music’s cognitive, emotional, and therapeutic impacts.

Our History

The Asian Music Psychology and Neuroscience Network was established in 2025, driven by the vision of creating a connected, dynamic research community exploring music's role in brain function and psychological processes. Beginning as a small collaboration among passionate researchers, the network quickly expanded to include labs and individual members throughout Asia, reflecting the region’s rich cultural diversity and academic strengths.
